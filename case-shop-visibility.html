Case Study · Machine Monitoring · Contract machine shop · Representative scenario

More jobs through the same shop.

The owner was quoting on gut feel, with no real picture of how much capacity the floor actually had.
More jobs. Same crew.Powered by SHARC

The situation

A growing contract machine shop was starting to turn work away — unsure whether the floor could absorb more. Production tracking was clipboards and end-of-day estimates, so the owner was bidding lead times and capacity on instinct.

The challenge

Without real-time utilization, the owner couldn't see idle capacity, justify a second shift, or promise realistic dates with confidence. Hiring more people felt like the only way to grow — an expensive bet when the existing machines might already have room.

The solution

Mr. IIoT put SHARC sensors on the machines, feeding a simple dashboard with live run time, utilization, and downtime — no five-figure OEM connectivity quote, no rip-and-replace of the controls.

You can't schedule against capacity you can't see. Once it's on a screen, the hidden hours show up fast.

The result

  • The owner can see real capacity and schedule against facts, not hunches.
  • Hidden idle time becomes visible — room to take on more work surfaces.
  • Lead-time promises get more accurate, and customers notice.
  • The shop pushes more throughput from the crew it already has before hiring.
